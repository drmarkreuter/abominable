How Long


On a cold February morning, I walked through my front doorway to find two pandas rearranging the furniture in my lounge, setting it up like a therapist's treatment room.
One Panda was lying on the sofa pretending to describe some childhood trauma, while the other Panda pretended to take notes and nodded wisely while gently sucking on a small stem of bamboo.
And I said “how long has this been going on?”

I continued my familiar yet alien journey into my kitchen where I found three baboons wearing lab coats gathered around a human-shaped form they had baked from different sizes of bread.
They stood ready to start a medical dissection of this 'breadman' using a selection of kitchen implements, including, but limited to the 10th anniversary special edition Jamie Oliver pizza slicer.
And I said “how long has this been going on?”

I climbed the stairs, pulling myself up on the wooden bannister and walked into the spare bedroom where a shaggy sloth was slowly unpicking the threads of an old yellow carpet and carefully weaving a nest from the tried yarn;
a nest that was slowly being filled with old paperback novels like a library for a flightless bird.
And I said “how long has this been going on?”

I escaped this madness by climbing the small wooden ladder into the attic space where I found seven badgers constructing a glider out of cardboard and bits of loft insulation.
The Chief Badger stopped momentarily to hand me a spare roll of parcel tape indicating some additional work needed on the tricky tail-section.
And I said… “how long has this been going on?”.

Bjork works in a Grocers


So I’m reliving my undergraduate days and I find myself queuing up in a small shop to get ingredients for a curry, which no doubt reflects my inability to complete this task during my waking hours.
This is one of those small shops that sells everything, but for some reason everything is behind the counter, and I seem to be queuing for ages, and who’s there working in the shop?

Bjork is in the grocers, She’s stood behind the counter. She’s selling people pickles, and lots of other goodies

So, I get to the counter and ask for a juice box, and she asks me which brand
And I say, I don’t mind any brand, and she says which brand.
And this little tete-e-tete continues for some time.
And I eventually get my little orange juice carton, which tells you it’s a dream, because I’d never buy that in real life.

Bjork is in the grocers, She’s stood behind the counter. She’s selling people pickles, and lots of other goodies

So I find myself asking: why is Bjork working in the Grocers?
Maybe she’s doing research for her new album much like actors research their next role
Also, I find it remarkable that I should dream about queuing and wonder if my brain is somehow reinforcing this British stereotype in a post-Brexit world
Also, I wonder what sort of curry I was going to make (because one British stereotype isn’t enough apparently).
